About

Engineer at the intersection of robotics, control, and industrial systems.

I'm a Robotics Engineer with hands-on experience in ROS2, Python, and C++ for autonomous systems. M.S. in Robotics & Autonomous Systems from Arizona State University; B.Tech in Electronics & Communications from NIT Andhra Pradesh.

At Hitachi America, I built control systems on WAGO field couplers, wired real-time monitoring dashboards over MQTT and Node-RED, and applied the Model Context Protocol (MCP) with Gemini agents and A2A orchestration to enhance quality-control processes.

Before that, I spent 18 months at ASU's BELIV lab building scenario-based tests for autonomous vehicles in CARLA-ROS-Autoware — work that turned into a co-authored paper at IEEE IV 2026 on real-time DDT assessment aligned with SAE J3237. More recently I've been working on learned control for legged robots, taking a reinforcement-learning locomotion policy from MuJoCo onto a Booster Robotics K1 humanoid, and running VR teleoperation on a Unitree G1 to collect bimanual demonstration data for imitation learning. I care about safety standards (ISO 26262 / 21448 / 34502, SAE J3016 / J3237), clean simulation pipelines, and writing code that someone else can debug at 2am.
